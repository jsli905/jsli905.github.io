Hollow Carbon Fiber Wheel

Lightweight composite manufacturing project focused on strength, weight reduction, and manufacturability

Overview

This project focused on designing and manufacturing a hollow carbon fiber wheel with the goal of maximizing stiffness while minimizing weight. The project required developing a manufacturable layup strategy, designing reusable molds, and validating the structural performance of the finished component.

Design Objectives

The main objective was to create a lightweight structural wheel using carbon fiber rather than traditional solid materials. The design needed to be strong enough to withstand loading while remaining hollow to reduce weight. This required careful consideration of laminate orientation, wall thickness, and mold design to ensure the part could be manufactured reliably.

My Contributions

  • Designed a hollow carbon fiber wheel structure focused on weight reduction and structural stiffness
  • Developed a manufacturable layup strategy and laminate schedule
  • Designed and built reusable molds for composite manufacturing
  • Performed the full carbon fiber layup process including cutting, layering, and curing
  • Refined the design based on manufacturability challenges during fabrication
  • Produced a finished hollow composite wheel and evaluated its structural performance

Manufacturing Process

The wheel was manufactured using layered carbon fiber sheets placed inside a custom mold. The design was intentionally hollow in order to reduce weight while still maintaining structural integrity. The manufacturing process required careful control of layer alignment, fiber orientation, and curing conditions to ensure a consistent and reliable result.

Carbon fiber layup and manufacturing process

Key Challenges

  • Designing a hollow structure that remained strong enough under load
  • Ensuring the carbon fiber layers maintained proper alignment during layup
  • Creating molds that could be reused while still producing a high-quality surface finish
  • Balancing weight reduction with structural reliability

Results

The final result was a hollow carbon fiber wheel that demonstrated significant weight reduction while maintaining structural strength. The project provided hands-on experience with composite design, mold creation, and manufacturing techniques, as well as insight into how material selection and laminate design affect mechanical performance.
